⚑  Key Principles — All Scenarios
💡 Embedded Licences
Where a company insists on rights to use for commercial purposes, this should be addressed as an embedded licence and not granted for free. Fully funded deals may justify an embedded licence or stronger option position than part-funded deals, where it should be considered exceptional.
🤝 Joint IP
Joint ownership terms must be explicitly defined in the agreement rather than relying on default legal rules. Patenting of Joint IP to be agreed between both parties. If the company holds priority rights (option or commercial licence), it should cover patent costs.
📋 Patenting
Patenting of University IP to be led by the University in consultation with the Company. Patenting of Joint IP to be agreed between the parties. If the company holds any priority rights (e.g. option or commercial licence), it should cover patent costs.
